<div id="mobile-incontent-social" data-width="12" data-height="12" data-ad="mobile-incontent-social"></div><div class="content-gallery-content"><div id="343581_0" class="current slide"><p>It's been over fifteen years since the <a href="https://comicbook.com/comics/news/dc-artist-is-putting-together-a-marvel-vs-dc-comic-on-twitter/" target="_self">last crossover between Marvel and DC was published in comic book form</a>, and in the years since things have changed drastically for both publishers. This perhaps makes it happening again a complicated affair, but we can all dream, and two of the high profile writers at both companies are eager to make it happen. One fan took to Twitter tonight to consider the idea of another crossover event, putting forward that writers <a href="/category/donny-cates/">Donny Cates</a> (<a href="https://comicbook.com/gaming/news/fortnite-donny-cates-defends-marvel-crossover/" target="_self">Thor, Venom, Guardians of the Galaxy</a>) and <a href="/category/scott-snyder/">Scott Snyder</a> (<a href="https://comicbook.com/anime/news/castlevania-scott-snyder-dc-comics-top-tier-netflix/" target="_self">Batman, Swamp Thing, Justice League</a>) should pen the event. Surprisingly, both agreed.</p>
<p>"I don't think anyone wants that to happen more than us! I know I'm down!" Cates wrote. Snyder replied quickly adding, "Seconded!" Fans quickly flocked to their answers, considering other ideas for writers that could lend their talents to the crossover and what artists they'd like to see take on the challenge. Take a peek at the responses below and let us know what you would want to see in a new <a href="/category/marvel-vs-dc/">Marvel vs DC</a>!</p>
<p>Marvel vs DC has long been a fan-favorite idea but it's only been pursued by the publishing companies a few times. The 1996 <em>DC vs. Marvel Comics </em>written by Peter David and Ron Marz with art by Dan Jurgens and Claudio Castellini was the first, featuring match-ups like Superman vs. Hulk, Spider-Man vs. Superboy, Batman vs. Captain America, Wolverine vs. Lobo, and Storm vs. Wonder Woman.</p>
<p>This was followed by the Marvel/DC combo series, the Amalgam Universe which included mash-ups like Princess Ororo of Themiscyra (Wonder Woman &amp; Storm), Dark Claw (Batman &amp; Wolverine), Spider-Boy (Superboy &amp; Spider-Man), Mister X (Martian Manhunter &amp; Professor X), and Ra's a-Pocalypse (Ra's al Ghul &amp; Apocalypse)</p>
<p>The second official crossover event took place in 2003/2004 with Kurt Busiek's <em>JLA/Avengers</em>, marking the last time that Marvel and DC allowed their characters to interact. This story featured the kind of moments fans craved in such an event like Superman wielding Mjolnir and Captain America's shield, both the DC and Marvel versions of "Captain Marvel" appearing in the same panels, and Batman admitting Captain America could probably beat him in a fight.</p>

<div id="343581_11"><h2 class="pcm-slideshow__title">Could it even happen?</h2>
<p>So could something like this happen? In the current environment, probably not. Former DC co-publisher Dan Didio squashed the idea of it at San Diego Comic-Con <a href="https://screenrant.com/dc-comics-marvel-crossover-never-happen/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">in 2017</a> calling the crossovers a "last resort," noting that they previously only happened because the comic book industry was in bad shape.</p>
<p>"Each company felt that they couldn't support themselves, they had to work together to create a product that created enough energy and interest for the fan base at the time," Didio said at the time. "I would rather put that creative energy just in our characters rather than working on a story with Marvel characters. And the shame of it, honestly, is there were certain things created in that mix-up, that mash-up, that were quite exciting but can never be revisited again without the cooperation between the two companies."</p>
<a class="mid-content-comment js-activateComments" data-key="343581" data-root="#comments" href="#"><span>2</span><span>comments</span></a><p>Though Didio is no longer part of the decision making at DC Comics, having been fired earlier this year, the likelihood of such a crossover still seems like a pipe dream. In the years since <em>JLA/Avengers</em>, Marvel was bought outright by The Walt Disney Company and the WB owned DC Comics is now property of AT&amp;T. For these two universes to even meet again it would require two major corporations and a lot of lawyers coming to agree on many terms.</p>
<p>Even if it can't happen, what would you like to see in a new version of a Marvel vs DC crossover? Should Lobo put the beat down on Wolverine this time? Could the Green Lanter get the better of the Silver Surfer? Will The Hulk take down Superman? Sound off with your thoughts below!</p>
