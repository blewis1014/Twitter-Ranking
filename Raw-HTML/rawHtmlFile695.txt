Like related types among the Qingzhou finds, this statute has a gently smiling face and meticulous detailing of splendid jewelry. A noticeable difference, however, is the unique <em>yingluo</em> (<em>mukt hara</em>) worn by the bodhisattva for personal adornment. The somewhat bizarre but imaginative combination of a string of jewels with grotesque animal masks, flying <em>apsara</em>, jade pendants, dragons, and metal ornaments was probably a regional decorative style of Shanxi Province.</p>
