<p id="paragraph-1">

<p>The reminder for the podcast keeps popping up like every five minutes, so let&rsquo;s work around this one last thing.</p>


<p id="paragraph-2">
<p>I&rsquo;m leaving The Gazette. It&rsquo;s been 24 years, nearly 22 covering Iowa football. That&rsquo;s a long damn time in the same circle against the same opponent. There can&rsquo;t be too many other beat writers who&rsquo;ve been on the griddle this long. If there are, what in the hell are you doing? You traded your heart for a phone.</p>


<p id="paragraph-3">
<p>Thanks to everyone who&rsquo;s been along for the ride. That&rsquo;s lame, I know, but we&rsquo;re not going to be dramatic here.</p>


<p id="paragraph-4">
<p>The On Iowa Podcast was sort of the ultimate &ldquo;Odd Couple&rdquo; thing. Scott Dochterman kept the records. I tagged along, learned a lot and had a lot of fun. I don&rsquo;t expect I&rsquo;ll be part of anything like it again unless I pull something fabulously entertaining out of my ass. I do plan to learn the electric guitar. The On Iowa pod worked because it was &ldquo;hyper-Iowa.&rdquo; There was nothing else, really. We didn&rsquo;t do guests. We did the Hawkeyes. You didn&rsquo;t get the &ldquo;drive by,&rdquo; you got a meal and a dessert and maybe a funny hat or an action figure. Scott is the smartest dude on the beat. I felt good in his company, during the podcast and out in stadiums. I was the &ldquo;Oscar,&rdquo; the Walter Matthau to Scott&rsquo;s &ldquo;Felix,&rdquo; the Jack Lemmon. But it wasn&rsquo;t always that way. This might not make sense, but I&rsquo;ll try: Where I was weak, Scott was strong and vice versa. That made for some really great content.</p>


<p id="paragraph-5">
<p>10 years and 2 &frac12; months worth, to be exact.</p>


<p id="paragraph-6">
<p>Yes, I&rsquo;m referencing a movie from 1968. I&rsquo;m no longer updating my references. I don&rsquo;t have to.</p>


	<div class="ad-block">

<p id="paragraph-7">
<p>We downsized and I moved to the woods across the river. That&rsquo;s something that&rsquo;s been in the mail for me since May and a big driving force behind this. Something something pandemic. Also, at some point, you have to stop bitching to your poor wife about your job. She&rsquo;s not your damn psychologist.</p>


<p id="paragraph-8">
<p>I&rsquo;m trading back for my heart. Face me to the shore. I&rsquo;m swimming to the good latitudes.</p>
