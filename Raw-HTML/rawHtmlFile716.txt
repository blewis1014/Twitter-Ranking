<div id="mobile-incontent-social" data-width="12" data-height="12" data-ad="mobile-incontent-social"></div><p>A top Disney investor wants the company's movies with blockbuster potential, including Marvel Studios' twice-delayed <em><a href="/category/black-widow-comicmovie/">Black Widow</a></em>, to bypass movie theaters for a streaming premiere on <a href="/category/disney-streaming-service/">Disney+</a>. Days before The Walt Disney Company <a href="https://comicbook.com/irl/news/disney-corporate-reorganization-places-more-focus-on-streaming-disney-plus-hulu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">announced a restructuring</a> that would immediately shift its "primary focus" to streaming via its Disney+, ESPN+, and Hulu platforms, the company sent Pixar's <em>Soul</em> straight-to-streaming for a Christmas Day release on Disney+ <a href="https://comicbook.com/movies/news/pixar-soul-disney-plus-no-fee-mulan-premier-access/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">without </a><a href="https://comicbook.com/movies/news/pixar-soul-disney-plus-no-fee-mulan-premier-access/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><em>Mulan</em></a><a href="https://comicbook.com/movies/news/pixar-soul-disney-plus-no-fee-mulan-premier-access/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">'s $29.99 Premier Access fee</a>. Dan Loeb, the hedge fund manager for Third Point with roughly a $1 billion stake in the company, says sending high-profile films like <em><a href="/category/black-widow-comicmovie/">Black Widow</a></em> to Disney+ would attract new subscribers while retaining current customers.</p><p>"What Netflix has is this immense subscriber base that allows it to invest in an enormous amount of content and amortize that to get more subscribers," Loeb told <a href="https://variety.com/2020/film/news/dan-loeb-disney-streaming-spending-1234796386/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><em>Variety</em></a>. "Disney isn't there yet, but they need to get there as quickly as possible. If they don't get critical mass in their subscriber base, they will be permanently disadvantaged versus Netflix."</p><p>Disney has already surpassed 60 million subscribers since launching Disney+ in November 2019. In August, Disney CEO Bob Chapek confirmed the streamer reached <a href="https://comicbook.com/movies/news/disney-60-million-subscribers-reached-5-years-early/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">its five-year goal</a> in just eight months, putting the service ahead of a forecast <a href="https://comicbook.com/tv-shows/news/disney-plus-users-forecast-2025/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">predicting 200 million subscribers</a> by 2025.</p><div class="modernInContent"><div id="mobile-native-plus-top1" class="mobile"></div></div><p>To move the streamer closer to Netflix's 193 million paid subscribers, Loeb urges Disney to skip theaters and instead send 2021-dated films like <em><a href="/category/black-widow-comicmovie/">Black Widow</a></em> to Disney+.</p><div class="modernInContent"><div id="native-plus-top1"></div></div><p>"My understanding is that the old-line executives don't want to go over the top with their big tentpole movies, which is why they announced they were pushing <em>Black Widow</em> and other movies to 2021," Loeb said. "I don't think they appreciate the tiger they have by the tail, which is to say the value they can drive by moving into a subscription model, which has been adopted by everyone from Microsoft to Amazon. It's so value accretive."</p><div class="modernInContent"><div id="mobile-native-plus-top2" class="mobile"></div></div><p>Loeb predicts theaters shuttered by the COVID-19 pandemic will survive but foresees them becoming more of a "novelty experience," saying the world is "going to go largely toward online distribution." Disney's October 12 reorganization is a step in that direction, but Chapek says the company will <a href="https://comicbook.com/movies/news/disney-will-still-release-movies-theatrically/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">continue theatrical releases</a> despite its focus on streaming.</p><p>"We've benefited from a tremendous relationship with theatrical exhibition for many, many, many years. As dynamics change in the marketplace, though, we want to make sure we're giving consumers, who want to go to theaters to experience everything that a theatrical release can give them, we want to continue to give them that option," Chapek said on CNBC's <em>Closing Bell</em>.</p><div class="modernInContent"><div id="native-plus-top2"></div><div id="mobile-native-plus-top3" class="mobile"></div></div><p>Acknowledging the growing number of consumers who wish to experience Disney films "from the safety, comfort, and convenience of their own home," he added, "We want to make sure that we put the consumer first, and the consumer is going to be making the decision in terms of how they consume our media as opposed to some arbitrary decision that we may make from a distribution standpoint. So we want to look at ourselves as consumer enablers."</p><a class="mid-content-comment js-activateComments" data-key="343579" data-root="#comments" href="#"><span>4</span><span>comments</span></a><p>Previously set for May 1 and then November 6, 2020, the Scarlett Johansson-starring <em>Black Widow</em> is now dated May 7, 2021. The delay makes 2020 the <a href="https://comicbook.com/movies/news/black-widow-delay-2020-first-year-without-marvel-studios-movie-since-2009/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">first year without a Marvel Studios film since 2009</a>.</p><p>Phase 4 of the Marvel Cinematic Universe launches with <em>WandaVision</em>, the first Marvel Studios series, premiering exclusively on Disney+ before the end of the year. A second season of the streamer's inaugural original series, <em>Star Wars</em> spinoff <em>The Mandalorian</em>, releases on October 30.</p><div class="modernInContent"><div id="mobile-native-plus-top4" class="mobile"></div></div>
